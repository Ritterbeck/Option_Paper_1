\begin{theorem} \label{eur_van_pde}
The partial differential equation of a European vanilla option is given by
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^{2} S^{2} \frac{\partial^{2} V}{\partial S^{2}}
    + bS \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} - rV = 0.
\end{equation*}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Step 1: Begin with geometric Brownian motion.
Step 2: Construct a portfolio
Step 3: Expand with Taylor series
Step 4: Substitute
Step 5: Choose Delta appropriately
Step 6: First form of PDE
Step 7: Substitute
Step 8: Rearrange
\end{proof}
